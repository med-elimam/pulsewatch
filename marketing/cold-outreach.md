# Cold outreach — email, DM, founder messages

Principles: personalize the first line to something real, lead with their pain not your product, one clear ask, no fake urgency, easy to say no. Never claim customers/revenue you don't have.

## Cold email (to small SaaS/agency founders)
**Subject:** how do you know your backups ran last night?

Hi {First},

Quick one — {Company} runs on scheduled jobs (backups, reports, syncs). Most teams your size don't find out one silently stopped until a customer or a failed restore tells them, because uptime monitors can't see a job that never ran.

I built Pulsewatch for exactly this: your job pings a URL when it finishes; if the ping doesn't arrive on schedule, you get an email/Slack alert. One line to set up, free for 5 monitors.

Worth a 2-minute look for your backup cron? Happy to send the link — or if it's not relevant, no worries at all.

{Your name}

## Cold DM (X / Slack / Discord — shorter)
Hey {First} — saw you run {thing} on a schedule. Built a tiny tool that pings you the moment a cron/backup stops checking in (the silent-failure case uptime monitors miss). One curl line, free tier. Want the link? No pressure.

## Reply-to-a-complaint DM (highest converting — respond to someone who just got burned)
Ran into your post about {the silent cron failure}. That exact thing is why I built Pulsewatch — a heartbeat monitor so a job that stops running actually alerts you instead of failing quietly. Free for a handful of monitors if you want to make sure it never bites you again: [link]. Either way, sorry that happened — it's the worst kind of bug.

## Founder-to-founder (warm-ish, e.g. in a builder community)
Hey — I just shipped Pulsewatch (cron/heartbeat monitoring, catches jobs that silently stop). I know you run {X}; would genuinely value 60 seconds of "does the onboarding make sense" feedback more than a signup. Mind taking a look? [link]

## Guardrails
- Send in small batches to people for whom it's genuinely relevant. This is outreach, not a blast.
- If a platform forbids cold DMs (many Discords/Slacks do), don't. Post in the designated self-promo/feedback channel instead.
- Track replies, iterate the subject line, stop anything that isn't landing.
